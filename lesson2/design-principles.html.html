Hick’s Law

Netflix

netlix.com

Nexflix is a good example of Hick’s Law. Nexflix has a huge database of movies and TV Shows. The user can spend a very long time searching just to find something to watch. Netfilx has designed its site to help the user out by doing a few things. First there is suggested videos. Then there are several tiers of categories to help narrow the options to a more manageable selection.

White Space and Clean Design

Google

google.com

Google has a very clean design and uses plenty of whitespace. Even though it is a powerful tool that can access every corner of the internet it manages to keep a clean and user-friendly design. The user is drawn right to the search field and does not have many other options to distract from the main purpose of the site.

Fitt’s Law

Amazon

amazon.com

Amazon applies Fitts Law throughout its website. First if you have not signed in yet the users focus goes right to a large bright yellow, easy to press button to sign in. Then the search bar and button are large and right on the top of the screen. Once the user finds and item, they want the pattern is repeated to get the user to easily purchase the item
